Why AIR Quality?

Air pollution, a silent killer lurking in the air we breathe, stands as a formidable threat to global health. Its insidious reach extends far beyond mere inconvenience, claiming an alarming number of lives each year. Shockingly, it ranks as the fourth leading cause of premature death worldwide, exacting a toll of nearly 7 million lives annually. This pervasive menace spares no one, with a staggering 99% of the global population subjected to its harmful effects, robbing individuals of an average of 2.3 years of life.

The health repercussions of air pollution are profound and far-reaching, affecting individuals from the earliest stages of life. From the womb to adulthood, virtually every organ in the human body bears the brunt of its toxicity. It triggers a myriad of acute and chronic illnesses, from cardiovascular and respiratory ailments to heightened risks of lung cancer, diabetes, and adverse birth outcomes.

Yet, the burden of this environmental scourge is far from evenly distributed. Vulnerable populations, including children and marginalized communities, bear the heaviest load. Children, with their rapid breathing rates and developing bodies, are especially susceptible, facing heightened exposure to pollutants that impair their growth and development. Meanwhile, marginalized groups endure disproportionate levels of pollution, compounded by broader social and environmental injustices perpetuated by systemic racism and historical inequalities.

In essence, air pollution not only threatens individual health but also serves as a stark reminder of the deep-rooted inequities that plague our societies. Addressing this crisis demands not only environmental action but also a concerted effort to tackle the underlying social and economic disparities that perpetuate its harmful effects.
