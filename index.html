Why AIR Quality?

Air pollution, a silent killer lurking in the air we breathe, poses a formidable threat to global health, extending far beyond mere inconvenience. Shockingly, it ranks as the fourth leading cause of premature death worldwide, claiming nearly 7 million lives annually. This pervasive menace spares no one, affecting 99% of the global population and robbing individuals of an average of 2.3 years of life.

The health repercussions of air pollution are profound, affecting individuals from the womb to adulthood. It triggers acute and chronic illnesses, from cardiovascular and respiratory ailments to heightened risks of lung cancer, diabetes, and adverse birth outcomes.

However, the burden of this environmental scourge is unevenly distributed, with vulnerable populations bearing the heaviest load. Children and marginalized communities are especially susceptible, facing heightened exposure and health risks. Addressing this crisis requires not only environmental action but also efforts to tackle the underlying social and economic disparities that perpetuate its harmful effects.
